Local context

Why frequent autonomous mowing fits Tyagarah

Tyagarah sits approximately 10 km north of Byron Bay in Byron Shire, NSW — coastal hinterland flats between Byron Bay and Brunswick Heads. The terrain is predominantly flat to gently undulating — sandier soils, large open paddocks, and blocks commonly run 1 to 5 acres.

The Northern Rivers climate is subtropical, with high summer rainfall that drives vigorous year-round grass growth. Common acreage grass types across Tyagarah — kikuyu, paspalum, couch and buffalo — all benefit from frequent mowing rather than a fortnightly contractor cycle: clippings stay short enough to mulch back in, the lawn never gets long enough to clump, and the property looks consistently presentable instead of saw-toothing between visits.
