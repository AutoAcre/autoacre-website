Local context

Why daily autonomous mowing fits Mullumbimby

Mullumbimby sits approximately 20 km north of Byron Bay in Byron Shire, NSW — a hinterland town surrounded by lifestyle and small-farm acreage. The terrain is mixed — flatter on the river plain, hilly on the hinterland fringe, and lifestyle blocks run from half-acre house blocks up to 10+ acres.

The Northern Rivers climate is subtropical, with high summer rainfall that drives vigorous year-round grass growth. Common acreage grass types across Mullumbimby — kikuyu, paspalum, couch and buffalo — all benefit from daily mowing rather than a fortnightly contractor cycle: clippings stay short enough to mulch back in, the lawn never gets long enough to clump, and the property looks consistently presentable instead of saw-toothing between visits.
