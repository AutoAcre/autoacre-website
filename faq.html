Autonomous mowing — frequently asked questions

Authoritative answers about autonomous acreage mowing in the Northern Rivers — costs, coverage, slope handling, safety, and how the AutoAcre Buy + Manage offer works.

About autonomous mowing

What is autonomous acreage mowing?

Autonomous acreage mowing is the use of a robotic mower that mows large rural and lifestyle properties on a daily, unattended schedule. Unlike a contractor visiting fortnightly with a ride-on, an autonomous mower operates every day, keeping grass at a consistent height year-round. AutoAcre delivers this as a Buy + Manage offer using the PANDAG G1 robotic mower across the Northern Rivers, NSW — customers own the mower outright and pay a monthly management fee for daily operation.

Why daily mowing instead of fortnightly?

Daily mowing keeps grass at a consistent length, which produces stronger turf, fewer weeds, and a manicured appearance every day. The clippings are short enough to mulch back into the soil, returning nutrients and reducing the need for fertiliser. Fortnightly mowing produces a saw-tooth pattern of growth that always looks tidy for a few days and overgrown for the rest.

What does AutoAcre cost compared to mowing the property myself?

Most acreage owners spend several hours per week mowing during the growing season, plus the cost of a ride-on, fuel, blades, servicing and storage. How does AutoAcre compare to a fortnightly mowing contractor?

A fortnightly contractor visits once every two weeks with a ride-on, leaving grass long for most of the cycle. AutoAcre mows daily with an autonomous unit, so grounds stay consistently manicured every day of the year. Contractors charge per visit; AutoAcre is a fixed monthly management fee that includes equipment, maintenance, and breakdown cover.

Coverage and capability

What size property does AutoAcre cover?

AutoAcre's Buy + Manage offer is designed for lifestyle acreage from 0.5 to 5 acres. For larger properties or commercial sites, the PANDAG G1 covers up to 25 acres per day. Larger estates, councils, schools, golf courses and solar farms are quoted as commercial deployments.

What slope can the PANDAG G1 mower handle?

The PANDAG G1 robotic mower used by AutoAcre handles slopes up to 38 degrees, which is steeper than most consumer robotic mowers and most commercial ride-on mowers. This makes it suitable for the Byron Bay hinterland, where many lifestyle properties have significant grade. AutoAcre assesses each property on-site to confirm slope coverage.

How many acres can an autonomous mower cover per day?

The PANDAG G1 covers up to 25 acres per day. For a typical 3-acre lifestyle property, this means the entire property can be mowed in a fraction of a day, and the rest of the day's capacity is used to keep growth even across zones.

What grass types does the autonomous mower handle?

AutoAcre's autonomous mowing handles all common Northern Rivers acreage grass types — including kikuyu, couch, paspalum, buffalo and rye blends. Daily mowing actually suits aggressive growers like kikuyu better than fortnightly contractors, because the grass never gets long enough to clump.

Does the autonomous mower work in rain or wet grass?

The PANDAG G1 can operate in light rain and on wet grass. In heavy weather, the unit pauses and resumes automatically once conditions allow. Because the mower runs daily rather than fortnightly, missing a single wet day has no visible impact on the property.

Does AutoAcre handle edges, garden beds, and trees?

AutoAcre's autonomous mowing handles the main mowing surface. Detail work — line trimming around trees, garden bed edges, and ornamental areas — is scoped during the on-site assessment and handled either by AutoAcre or by an existing gardener, depending on the customer's preference.

Safety, security and reliability

Is the autonomous mower safe around children and pets?

Yes. The PANDAG G1 has multiple safety systems including collision detection, lift sensors, and GPS-bounded operation. It operates at low speeds and stops immediately on contact with an obstacle. AutoAcre configures exclusion zones for play areas, garden beds, pools and other sensitive areas during installation.

Will the autonomous mower disturb my neighbours?

The PANDAG G1 runs significantly quieter than petrol ride-on mowers — closer to the volume of a household appliance. Daily operation typically goes unnoticed by neighbours, and operating hours can be configured to avoid early mornings or late evenings.

Is the autonomous mower secure from theft?

The PANDAG G1 has GPS tracking, geofencing, and a PIN-locked operating system. If the unit is moved off the property without authorisation, it locks itself and AutoAcre is notified. Theft of working autonomous mowers is rare because they are useless without the dock and the operating account.

What happens if the autonomous mower breaks down?

Repair coordination is included in the management service. If the unit needs attention, AutoAcre coordinates repair or warranty replacement — typically within seven days. Because customers own the mower outright, replacement decisions remain with the owner. Ad-hoc on-site call-outs (for example, manual mowing while a unit is out of service) are billed at $150 per visit.

How long does the autonomous mower last?

Customers own the PANDAG G1 outright, so end-of-life replacement is ultimately the owner's decision. AutoAcre coordinates servicing, repairs and warranty work through the management service, and advises on replacement timing when a unit reaches end-of-life. Commercial-grade builds like the PANDAG G1 typically last many years of daily operation.

Operations and onboarding

Where does the autonomous mower live on the property?

The PANDAG G1 docks at a small charging station that AutoAcre installs in an unobtrusive spot — typically near a shed, garage or boundary. The dock requires standard mains power and a small footprint. Installation is included as part of the Buy + Manage onboarding.

Do I need to be home for the mower to operate?

No. The autonomous mower operates without supervision. AutoAcre's customers include absentee owners, holiday rental managers, and people who travel for work. The management service provides ongoing operational visibility, and weekly photo reports are available on the higher tier (quote-only) for owners who want a regular visual record of the property.

What are the weekly photo reports?

Each week AutoAcre sends customers a short report with current photos of the property, the mower's operating status, and any maintenance notes. This is particularly valuable for absentee owners and short-stay rental managers who need to confirm presentation without driving out to the property.

How does the on-site demo work?

AutoAcre brings the PANDAG G1 to your property and runs a demonstration on your actual terrain. This confirms slope handling, mowing patterns and coverage on the property as it really is — not on a flat showroom lawn. The demo fee is credited to the first month if you proceed with the Buy + Manage offer.
