Why the generic mowing advice doesn't fit here

Most under-tree mowing guides are written for orchards in drier climates. The Northern Rivers wet season changes the calculus — groundcover grows faster, mud bogs lighter equipment, and the window between "fine" and "fire risk" is shorter than the textbook suggests.

This guide skips the textbook stuff and goes to what actually matters here: when to mow, what to plant under the trees, how to stay on the right side of NSW RFS, and what equipment specs hold up in this climate.

Wet season growth is aggressive. You'll mow more often from December through March than growers further west.

Who it's for

Macadamia growers around Byron, Bangalow, Alstonville, Ballina, Lismore and the wider Northern Rivers. Useful whether you're managing one block or a multi-property operation, and whether you currently mow yourself, contract it out, or are weighing autonomous options.
