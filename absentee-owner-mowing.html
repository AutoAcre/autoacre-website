What absentee owners actually need from a mowing service

Visibility without being there

The hardest part of owning a Northern Rivers property from Sydney isn't the mowing — it's not knowing what the property currently looks like. AutoAcre's weekly photo report replaces that with a short email each week. Current grounds, mower status, anything you need to know. Owners going months between physical visits know what they'd see if they drove up.

Zero coordination from interstate

With a contractor, every rebooking is a phone call, every wet weekend is a reschedule, every breakdown is your problem to solve from another city. With AutoAcre, the mower runs every day without supervision and AutoAcre handles every operational issue. The owner's involvement after onboarding is essentially zero.

The grounds look ready when you arrive

Driving up for a weekend or holiday and finding the lawn overgrown is the small disappointment most absentee owners have learned to expect from contractor mowing. Frequent autonomous mowing closes that gap completely — every arrival sees the property at its best, regardless of when the last contractor visit happened to fall.

Coexists with the local people you already trust

AutoAcre handles bulk mowing. Existing arrangements — gardener for detail work, neighbour for property checks, property manager for rentals — continue unchanged. Many absentee owners find their gardener becomes more useful when they're not burning hours on the lawn itself, and the property looks consistently better between gardener visits.

Reports go to the people who need them

The weekly photo report can be sent to multiple addresses — the owner, a property manager, a family member who lives closer. Overseas owners often CC the local manager so everyone has the same view of presentation.

Breakdown cover that's not on you

If the autonomous unit fails, AutoAcre's repair coordination service kicks in: repair or replacement within a week, with a zero-turn ride-on dispatched at no extra cost in the meantime. The owner doesn't make a single phone call. The property stays maintained while AutoAcre runs the response.

Common questions from absentee owners

How do absentee owners know the property is being maintained?

Weekly photo report — current photos of the property, the mower's operating status, and any maintenance notes. The verification that grounds look right, without anyone driving out to confirm.

Do I need someone on the ground to coordinate?

No. AutoAcre handles everything operationally. The owner's only ongoing involvement is reading the weekly photo report. Many absentee owners go months without speaking to AutoAcre.

What about contractors and gardeners I already use?

AutoAcre coexists with everything else. The mower covers bulk mowing; existing arrangements for detail, garden beds, pool, property checks continue.

What if something breaks while I'm in Sydney?

repair coordination. Repair or replacement within a week. Zero-turn ride-on plus operator dispatched at no extra cost in the meantime. You don't coordinate anything.

Set up for properties I rent part-time?

Yes — many customers split between personal use and short-stay rentals. Frequent mowing means the property is always presentable for both, with no scrambling before either.

What if I sell the property?

The mower is owned outright and stays with the property unless taken. The management service is month-to-month and transferable — new owners can continue, transfer, or end with reasonable notice. Many find existing AutoAcre management makes the property more appealing to buyers.

How does access work when I'm away?

Initial installation requires one site visit; after that the mower operates continuously without access. Physical service (blade replacement etc.) is set up during onboarding — typically a coded gate, a neighbour, or an existing property manager.

Can photo reports go to a property manager or family member?

Yes. Reports can go to multiple email addresses. Many absentee owners CC their local manager.
